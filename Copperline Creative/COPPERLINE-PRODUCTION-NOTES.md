Copperline Creative Website Direction Brainstorm

Working purpose of this document

This is the thinking space for defining what the Copperline Creative website should actually be before handing anything back to Lumen.

No final build prompts yet. This file is for shaping the creative direction, layout rules, required sections, and asset usage until the vision feels clear.

Current problem

Lumen understands the words “bento layout,” but keeps interpreting it in one of two wrong ways:

A normal long-form website chopped into squares.

A chaotic floating rectangle collage.

Neither direction feels right for Copperline Creative.

The goal is not simply “more squares.” The goal is a curated, purposeful, visual-first homepage that feels like a polished creative studio board.

Emerging direction

Copperline Creative should feel like:

A polished creative studio desk

A brand board

A portfolio collage

A warm, editorial mosaic

A visual proof-piece for the service itself

The homepage should show taste, clarity, warmth, and practical creativity immediately.

It should not feel like:

A generic template

A standard stacked homepage

A corporate agency site

A random masonry board

A scrapbook of decorative assets

A long page pretending to be bento

Core design phrase

Controlled mosaic, not chaotic masonry.

The layout should be visually interesting, but still aligned, intentional, readable, and calm.

Bento / mosaic cards when used well

Bento-style cards can work beautifully for Copperline when they are purposeful, grouped, and visually connected.

Important clarification:

Not every card needs to be a square.

Cards can be squares, rectangles, wide strips, tall feature panels, or small tiles.

Square clusters should be grouped intentionally rather than scattered randomly across the page.

The layout should feel like a curated composition, not loose blocks floating around.

Bento should create rhythm and interest, not confusion.

Dark and light contrast

The dark/light contrast is working well and should be used more confidently throughout the site.

Strong examples:

Dark footer or CTA areas against the warm ivory page

Dark/blackened feature cards

The main top-left logo/paint-smear brand moment

Light cards beside dark cards for contrast and visual rhythm

The site should use more dark and light contrast throughout, especially to make the original paint-smear logo and dark brand cards feel intentional and powerful.

Specific reference elements to keep

The following reference-style elements feel right:

Original Copperline logo with black paint smear as a major brand moment

Slim three-part values strip

Dark “Hi, I’m Carley” about card

Portfolio image squares / transitional project tiles

Visual-led cards rather than too many text-only slogan cards

Current dislikes

Remove unnecessary text squares

The recent layout introduced too many standalone text squares/cards. This is not working.

Remove the following style of cards unless they are part of a clearly defined section:

random slogan cards

process cards floating in the main mosaic

“start here” cards sitting in the middle of the design

generic proof/value text boxes

filler cards created just to occupy space

The only large standalone dark/black square/card that should remain in this part of the layout is the strong brand/logo card using the original paint-smear logo.

The layout should not become a collection of disconnected text boxes. Text should be consolidated into purposeful areas: hero intro, services, about, portfolio captions, values strip, and contact CTA.

Hero logo problem

The site must showcase the actual original Copperline logo on black with the paint smear as a major hero/brand element.

The paint-smear logo is one of the strongest Copperline assets and should not be replaced with a cleaner or weaker logo treatment in the main hero area.

The top-left / main brand card should make the dramatic logo feel like the centrepiece of the site.

Bento misuse

The current bento attempts feel like either:

Spruce & Spirit-style long-form sections with more squares, or

chaotic floating rectangle soup

Need more structure and less interpretation.

Random graphics

Graphics should not be placed just because they exist.

Every asset needs a job.

Services icon treatment

The service icons are useful, but the current treatment is not working when they are placed inside unnecessary square boxes.

Avoid putting service icons inside awkward square picture frames or decorative boxes that make them feel like pasted-in images.

Preferred service icon treatment:

Use icons as clean line-style accents.

Place them inside small circles, soft badge shapes, or directly beside headings.

Keep them aligned and consistent.

Do not make the icon container compete with the card content.

The service cards should feel polished, not like the icons were dropped into generic image placeholders.

Doodles

Doodles are not currently working when used as large card graphics or random decoration.

Use only if:

They are truly transparent PNGs

They are small

They support a heading, button, or gentle moment

They do not look pasted on

Do not use large phrase doodles as main card content.

Paint-smear logo underuse

The original Copperline logo with the black paint smear is one of the strongest brand assets and must be used intentionally.

It should not sit unused in the folder.

The line “Designer, dreamer, and your biggest cheerleader.” is loved, but it is not the right line for a large oversized square/card in the current layout. When placed in a very large dark square, it looks accidental and visually underfilled.

Use this line more intentionally:

As a smaller personal/about card.

As a supporting introduction, not a huge empty feature block.

With enough surrounding content or visual detail to justify the card size.

Potentially as part of a dedicated About section rather than a dominant first-screen block.

Portfolio direction

The current portfolio treatment is not working and should be removed from the main mosaic in its current form.

Problems with the current portfolio attempt:

Too many screenshots layered together.

Project images feel crammed and confusing.

Text overlays are competing with the screenshots.

The portfolio area feels like a busy collage rather than a polished proof section.

The work is not being showcased clearly or beautifully.

We need to figure out a different way to showcase portfolio work.

Preferred revised direction:

Remove the crowded stacked screenshot collage.

Do not use multiple overlapping screenshots as the main portfolio treatment.

Do not use overlay text across the portfolio images/squares. This is not the desired look.

Do not use large grey, black, or translucent caption blocks sitting on top of project screenshots.

Do not place project screenshots in a way that crops important text awkwardly.

Portfolio should feel curated, calm, and image-led.

Portfolio captions should sit clearly outside the image tile, either beneath the image, beside the image, or in a separate clean text area. The image itself should be allowed to breathe.

Possible alternatives to explore:

Clean recent work strip

Three equal project tiles.

Each tile uses a consistent square or near-square crop.

Project name and short service type underneath.

Minimal hover effect only.

Featured project card plus smaller thumbnails

One larger featured project image.

Two or three smaller supporting project tiles.

Clear hierarchy and breathing room.

Device/mockup-led portfolio

Use one laptop/phone mockup per project.

Keep each project self-contained.

Avoid layering too many screens at once.

Portfolio preview carousel/slider later

Could be considered later, but do not add unnecessary complexity yet.

Potential projects:

Copperline Creative

Teegz / placeholder if suitable

Important rule:

The portfolio section should prove the work, not overwhelm the visitor.

Contact CTA / footer direction

The current footer is not working and needs a full rethink.

Current footer problems:

It did not change enough from the earlier version.

It feels like a generic dark contact block rather than a Copperline brand moment.

The white contact box inside the dark footer feels disconnected from the mood of the rest of the site.

The layout is too literal: big text on the left, contact box on the right.

It does not have the elegant branded CTA feeling from the reference.

The footer should not look like an afterthought or a standard business contact panel.

Preferred direction:

The footer/contact CTA should feel more like a designed brand strip or closing card, similar in spirit to the reference CTA:

Let’s create something beautiful together.

Button:

Get in touch →

Design notes:

Use a dark painterly purple/charcoal background or blackened plum card.

The CTA should feel elegant, simple, and branded.

Use copper accents and/or subtle copper speckles.

Avoid a large white box floating inside the footer.

Avoid making the footer feel like a generic contact form/card.

Keep the design flatter, more integrated, and more atmospheric.

The dark background should carry the Copperline texture and mood.

The button can sit directly on the dark background or inside a very subtle integrated panel.

Practical contact details can appear underneath or below the CTA in a quieter footer row.

The main visual moment should be the branded call-to-action, not a contact-detail box.

Possible footer structure:

Dark painterly CTA strip/card

Left or centred handwritten/serif CTA text:
Let’s create something beautiful together.

Copper/black button:
Get in touch →

Subtle copper speckles or paint texture

Quiet practical footer row underneath

Email

Phone

Navigation links

Copyright / business name

The CTA and footer should feel connected to the hero and dark brand cards through dark/light contrast, copper accents and subtle texture.

Copperline needs to feel custom, thoughtful, warm, practical, and visually memorable.
